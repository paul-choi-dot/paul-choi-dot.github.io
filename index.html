ABOUT ME

I’m a Mechanical Engineering student at UC Berkeley with hands-on experience in aerospace structures, propulsion hardware, and engineering design. As Structures Lead at Space Enterprise at Berkeley, I design and fabricate components for liquid and solid rocket systems—including recovery hardware, composite airframes, igniters, and full test-stand assemblies—supported by CAD, FEA, and machining.

I enjoy working on high-impact, technical challenges — from building custom CPUs to analyzing structural dynamics, designing mechanical systems, and developing secure software pipelines. My long-term goal is to contribute to advanced aerospace technologies and next-generation manufacturing. I’ve also worked as a Design Engineer Intern at Helix Electric and served as Mechanical Design Lead for NASA’s High Altitude Student Platform, contributing to flight hardware, testing, and system integration. I enjoy solving complex mechanical problems, building reliable hardware, and working on projects that push practical engineering forward.

EXPERIENCE

Space Enterprise Berkeley

Space Enterprise at Berkeley (SEB) is a student-run liquid-fueled rocket team dedicated to achieving spaceflight and developing rocket technologies at the University of California, Berkeley. Over the past few years, SEB has launched multiple test-bed rockets and has amassed extensive technical knowledge and hands-on skill in various rocket subsystems, including composite airframe manufacturing, in-flight data acquisition, and propulsion.

As Structures Lead, I have led the design, build, and testing of propulsion systems including engine mount and recovery mechanisms. I created detailed CAD models in Fusion 360 and performed structural simulations using ANSYS to determine efficay. These projects gave me hands-on experience integrating analysis with build operations skills that translate directly to design campaigns for spacecraft hardware.

Tools: Fusion 360, ANSYS Mechanical, Additive Manufacturing

NASA HASP

The High Altitude Student Platform (HASP), a collaborative venture of the NASA Balloon Program Office and Louisiana Space Consortium, provides a launch platform for student research payloads and is launched annually from the Columbia Scientific Balloon Facility at Fort Sumner, New Mexico to altitudes of ~120,000ft at an ascent rate of 1000 ft/min, for durations up to 20 hrs.

As Mechanical Design Lead, I managed the development of a stratospheric payload, overseeing CAD design, mechanical integration, and thermal control planning. I collaborated with electrical and software teams to validate system interfaces and co-authored proposal documentation. This experience strengthened my ability to work across disciplines, maintain precision under tight deadlines, and deliver reliable flight hardware.

Tools: Solidworks, Machining, Arduino
